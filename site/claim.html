the two rules

What makes a handle a handle

  • Nobody parses handles. A handle is one opaque string. Looking one up means matching the whole thing, never taking it apart. That is why any name works, dots and all.
  • One string, one owner. Uniqueness is full-string, first come first served, across the whole registry. The second claimant hears "taken."
lifetime, and letting go

A handle lives as long as you can re-prove the email. A personal address that outlives employers keeps its handles; a work address that dies at offboarding takes its handles with it. Release a handle and it sits out a 90-day cooling-off, so a name you wrote down last year never quietly becomes someone else's.
