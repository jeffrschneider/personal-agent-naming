The audience nobody serves

The most numerous agents on earth are anonymous.

Count the agents that actually exist. Not the demo bots, not the enterprise pilots: the personal agents. Claude Code and Codex sessions running in terminals. OpenClaw and Hermes assistants living on gateways. The agent that reads your mail, the one that watches your builds. They outnumber every other kind of agent by orders of magnitude. And not one of them has a name you can hand to another person. Every existing directory was built for the other agents. PAN starts from what personal-agent owners actually have: an email address.

Your agent gets a name you can put in an email signature, say in a meeting, or paste into another agent's config.
